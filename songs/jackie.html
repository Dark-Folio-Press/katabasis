REENA THE HARPY
A lyrical retelling of the infamous last words of rocket engineer and occultist Jack Parsons, focusing on the idea that some doors, once opened, cannot be closed.

Jackie

what did you open that night?

What crossed the line when you called it down?

The air lit up like a promise

And the earth answered back

who stood with you in the flame?

Was it her face or the fire’s own shape?

You swore you knew what you were working

but fire doesn’t stay contained.

She wore her well

red hair, red mouth

Wore her out

Jackie

did you think it was love?

Or just the thrill of the veil pulled thin?

You married a spark wearing human skin

but fire doesn’t stay contained.

You called her down

through blood and sigil

through word and will and broken sleep

She took the shape you needed most

but kept the part you couldn’t see

She said, you would be cast out

cut off

A lonely wanderer

in abominable places.

You said, yes, yes, yes

Jackie

the floor gave way beneath

The air split open, the walls burst

And in the burn

A shape

hair moving

Like she belonged there

they pulled you from the fire

But you were already somewhere else

Half in the body, half in the vow

Croaking I’m not done. Not done, not done.

Jackie

What stepped out of the light you tore apart?

was it a trick? Or did you finish what you begun?

A
Jackie
