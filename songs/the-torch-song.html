The Torch Song

REENA THE HARPY
Part warning, part invitation, part promise. This song imagines Liberty as an ancient witness who has watched wealth, power, migration, corruption, and empires rise and fall and who still carries a torch for the hopeful.

No, really.

Look up.

I've been standing here

before the skyline.

Before the freeways.

Before the billionaires.

Before every man

who thought he could buy this land.

I see you.

The tired.

The hungry.

The loud.

The scared.

I know your names.

And I know theirs too.

The ones selling fear for profit

and calling cages freedom.

Don't think I missed it.

I carry a torch.

For the lost.

For the hopeful.

For the ones crossing dark water

looking for a shore.

For a victorious cocktail

at the end of a long day

A torch lights the way,

and burns it down

too

I am the light

that won't shut off.

I am the flood

that climbs the banks.

The wave

that takes the dock.

The wildfire

crossing county lines.

I am the bear

at the edge of the treeline.

The mountain lion

watching from the rocks.

I don't chase.

I wait.

I was here before the towers.

Before the borders.

Before the money.

Before the deals.

And yeah.

I saw that too.

The counterfeit kings.

The seat-stealers.

The freedom-talkers

with chains hidden in their pockets.

I see every one of them.

I know every lie.

Every handshake.

Every backroom bargain.

Every promise sold cheap.

You think darkness protects you?

Baby,

I carry a torch.

You think height protects you?

I got mountains.

You think walls protect you?

Ask every town

that thought the river was bluffing.

I rise,

people notice.

I speak,

markets shudder.

I point,

regimes fall

and empires shake.

The torch shows everything.

Seek courage.

Because truth's got claws.

Truth's got teeth.

Truth's got paws

the size of dinner plates.

Truth walks slow.

Like a bear crossing the highway.

Everybody sees it.

Nobody stops it.

I didn't come here

to welcome tyrants.

I came here

to make them irrelevant.

So stand up.

Raise your head.

Hold your ground.

The flood is rising.

The fire is spreading.

That's what a torch is for.
